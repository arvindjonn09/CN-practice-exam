WAN — Wide Area Networks

⏱ ~30 min to read
🔬 Lab included
❓ 5-question quiz
Step 1 — Understand the Concept
A WAN (Wide Area Network) connects devices across large geographical areas — cities, countries, even continents. Unlike a LAN that you own and manage yourself, a WAN usually relies on service provider infrastructure (like ISP leased lines, fiber, 4G/5G, or satellite) to carry your traffic between sites.
🏙️
What connects over a WAN?
Branch offices, data centers, remote workers, cloud services. Anything that needs to talk across geographic distance.
💸
Who owns the WAN?
You typically rent bandwidth from an ISP. Your router connects to their edge — you control your LAN, they control the WAN core.
📉
WAN vs LAN — key difference
LAN = fast, cheap, you own it. WAN = slower, expensive per Mbps, provider-managed. WANs have higher latency because of distance.
🔧
Common WAN technologies
Leased lines (T1/E1), MPLS, Metro Ethernet, DSL, Cable, SD-WAN (modern software-defined), 4G/5G LTE backup.

Draw this — WAN topology

HQ Office (LAN) Branch Office (LAN) ┌─────────────────┐ ┌─────────────────┐ │ PC ─ Switch ─ Router ─────WAN────── Router ─ Switch ─ PC │ │ 192.168.1.x │ (ISP leased) │ 192.168.2.x │ └─────────────────┘ └─────────────────┘ │ Internet / ISP Cloud / Data Center

Remember these before the lab

  • WANs use serial or Ethernet interfaces to connect to the provider
  • The router's WAN port faces the ISP; the LAN port faces your switch
  • WAN links have a DCE/DTE relationship — one end sets the clock rate
  • Default gateway on every PC = the LAN-side IP of the router
  • Troubleshoot with: show ip interface brief, ping, traceroute

Step 2 — Hands-on Lab
Lab Goal: Connect two routers over a WAN serial link in Packet Tracer. Configure IP addresses, enable the interfaces, and verify connectivity with ping.
🗺️
Topology to build
R1 (Serial0/0/0) ←—serial link—→ (Serial0/0/0) R2
R1 LAN: 192.168.1.1/24 · R2 LAN: 192.168.2.1/24
WAN link: 10.0.0.0/30
💻
Commands you'll use
interface serial 0/0/0
ip address 10.0.0.1 255.255.255.252
clock rate 64000
no shutdown
