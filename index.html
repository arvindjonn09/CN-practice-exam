# The 5-Level Roadmap

Each level has its own page with the full exam syllabus, a topic checklist that saves your progress, hands-on labs mapped to real-world scenarios, and the simulators/tools to practice on.

CCST

1 · Cisco Certified Support Technician — Absolute Beginner

The entry point to get your foot in the door of IT. No prerequisites, no experience needed. Proves you can support real networks on day one of a help-desk job.

Target roles: Help Desk, IT Support Technician, NOC Trainee

→
CCNA

2 · Cisco Certified Network Associate — Associate

The classic foundation for networking. Teaches the underlying logic of how the entire internet works — valued even by cloud giants like Amazon and Microsoft.

~$75,000 average US starting salary

→
CCNP

3 · Cisco Certified Network Professional — Professional

Where you specialize. The Automation track (former DevNet) has seen a 25% jump in job listings — networking + Python + APIs is the hottest combo in infrastructure.

Target roles: Network Engineer, Network Automation Engineer

→
CCIE

4 · Cisco Certified Internetwork Expert — Expert

The "fighter jet pilot" of the networking world. An 8-hour hands-on lab exam that fewer than 3% of network engineers ever pass.

Up to $170,000 (security) · ~75% salary premium over non-certified peers

→
CCAr

5 · Cisco Certified Architect — Architect

The ultimate level. An elite club of fewer than 1,000 people globally. Less about configuration, more about business strategy — the track for future CTOs.

Target roles: Network Architect, Principal Engineer, CTO track

→

# My Learning System

A certificate is the milestone, not the goal — real growth comes from a deliberate learning system. These six habits are baked into every page of this site.

1. Learn in Public

  • This repo is my learning journal. Every lab I complete gets committed here — Packet Tracer files, configs, topology diagrams, write-ups.
  • Writing up what I built forces me to explain it clearly — the Feynman technique. If I can't write it simply, I haven't learned it yet.
  • Green commit squares keep me honest about consistency.

2. Lab-First, Not Video-First

  • Watching a video feels like learning; configuring it yourself IS learning. Hands-on time should be at least 40% of total study time.
  • Break things on purpose: every working lab gets deliberately sabotaged, then repaired. Troubleshooting is where understanding is built.
  • Rebuild old labs from a blank config — if I can't, I memorized instead of learned.

3. Spaced Repetition & Review

  • Anki flashcards 15 min/day for facts (port numbers, AD values, timers) — memory is a system, not luck.
  • The topic checklists on every cert page track what I've truly covered — reviewed weekly, honestly.
  • Monthly: redo one old lab from scratch to fight the forgetting curve.

4. Think in Real-World Scenarios

  • Every lab on this site ends with a real-world case — because knowledge tied to a story sticks, and trivia evaporates.
  • For each topic I ask: "what ticket, outage or project would this show up in?" That question turns facts into intuition.
  • It also means that when I do face the real thing, I've already rehearsed it.

5. Stack Skills, Not Just Certs

  • CCNA + Linux basics + Python grows me faster than CCNA alone — skills compound.
  • From CCNP onward, automation (Netmiko, Ansible, REST APIs) deepens everything else I've learned.
  • Cloud networking (AWS VPC / Azure VNet) makes the same concepts click from a second angle: on-prem + cloud.

6. Apply While Learning

  • Use each level in the real world before chasing the next one. Real tickets and projects cement theory like nothing else.
  • Study level N+1 while practicing level N — experience and study reinforce each other instead of competing.
  • Teach what I learn — explaining a concept to someone else is the final exam for whether I actually understand it.

# The Toolbox (Used Across All Levels)

Full details on each cert page — this is the master list of simulators and tools this whole journey runs on.

Cisco Packet Tracer

Cisco's official simulator. Perfect for CCST and CCNA — drag-and-drop topologies, real IOS commands, built-in assessment activities.

Free (Cisco Networking Academy)

GNS3

Emulates real Cisco IOS images. The step up from Packet Tracer — full command set, integrates with real VMs and Wireshark.

Free & open source

EVE-NG

Professional network emulation platform. The standard for CCNP/CCIE-scale topologies with dozens of devices. Community edition is free.

Free (Community) / Paid (Pro)

Cisco Modeling Labs (CML)

Cisco's official virtual lab platform with legal, current IOS-XE/NX-OS images. What the actual CCIE lab exam runs on.

~$199/yr (Personal)

Wireshark

Packet capture and analysis. Watching a TCP handshake or an OSPF hello in Wireshark turns theory into intuition. Used at every level.

Free & open source

Cisco DevNet Sandbox

Free cloud-hosted real Cisco gear (Catalyst, SD-WAN, ACI) with API access. Essential for the automation topics from CCNA onward.

Free (developer.cisco.com)

Python + Netmiko / Ansible

The automation stack: Python with Netmiko/NAPALM for device scripting, Ansible for config management, Git for everything.

Free & open source

Subnetting Practice

subnettingpractice.com and subnetting.org — daily drills until you can subnet in under 15 seconds. Non-negotiable for CCNA.

Free
